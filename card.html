Penguins

They live almost exclusively in the Southern Hemisphere. Only one species, the Galapagos penguin, is found in the Northern Hemisphere.

  • Scientific Name: Spheniscidae
  • Average Length: 3.8 feet tall
  • Average Lifespan: 20 years
  • Habitat: Variety of climates and regions

Highly adapted for life in the water, penguins have counter shaded dark and white plumage, and their wings have evolved into flippers. Most penguins feed on krill, fish, squid and other forms of sea life which they catch while swimming underwater.
